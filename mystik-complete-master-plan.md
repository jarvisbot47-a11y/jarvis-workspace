## 9. THE 4 DEAL TYPES — KNOW THEM BEFORE YOU SIGN
*(From: Top 4 Music Deals — ozHgwg9-NBk, Label Conversations — wOIDpniXfXs)*

NLN breaks down every deal type:

**Type 1: Distribution Deal (P&D — Pressing & Distribution)**
- What it is: They get your music on Spotify, Apple, etc. You keep 100% ownership.
- Cost: $20-50/year through DistroKid, Tunecore, CDBaby
- NLN verdict: EVERY independent artist should have this. Non-negotiable baseline.

**Type 2: Development Deal**
- What it is: Label spends money marketing you, takes a percentage of your revenue
- Risk: They often want ownership of your catalog or publishing
- NLN verdict: Only take if they can answer: "What will you specifically do for me in the first 90 days?"

**Type 3: Hybrid Deal**
- What it is: Shared rights, shared costs, split revenue
- NLN verdict: Viable if terms are fair. Watch for 360 clauses.

**Type 4: Traditional Label Deal**
- What it is: They own your masters, pay you a royalty, control everything
- NLN verdict: Almost never worth it for artists under 100K monthly listeners

**The number one rule (NLN): Never sign away your catalog. Your catalog is your long-term income stream. Once it's gone, you're working for someone else forever.**

# ═══════════════════════════════════════
# THE DEAL STRUCTURE PLAYBOOK (NLN)
# ═══════════════════════════════════════

*(From: Top 4 Music Deals, What Happens When A Label Reaches Out)*

**Distribution Deal (P&D):**
- Used by: DistroKid, Tunecore, CDBaby, ONErpm
- You own 100% of your music and publishing
- They take 0-15% of streaming revenue depending on tier
- You keep all catalog rights forever
- **Use this. Always.**

**Development Deal:**
- Label spends $5K-$50K on your marketing
- In exchange: 20-50% of your streaming revenue for 1-3 years
- Often comes with conditions on release schedule, approval rights
- **Only take if:** they can prove reach into editorial playlists, radio, or sync licensing you can't access alone

**360 Deal:**
- Label takes a percentage of ALL revenue streams: streaming, touring, merch, publishing, sync
- Designed for major label A-list artists
- **Unless you're being offered millions, this is a terrible deal for an indie**

**The question to ask every label:**
*"What will you specifically do for me in the first 90 days? Give me names, numbers,playlist placements, marketing spend commitments."*

If they can't answer specifically, walk away.
